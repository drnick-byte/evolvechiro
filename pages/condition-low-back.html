The Problem

Why low back pain keeps coming back

Low back pain is the leading cause of disability in the United States — and most of it goes undertreated. Not because the treatments don't exist, but because most providers chase the symptom instead of the source.

If you've tried rest, stretching, or over-the-counter pain relievers and found yourself back in the same place a few weeks later, that's why. Pain relief and structural correction are not the same thing.

At Pinnacle Chiropractic, we start with a thorough evaluation — including postural analysis, orthopedic testing, neurological screening, and digital X-rays when indicated — to find what's actually driving your pain. Then we build a care plan around correcting it.

Common causes we treat

  • Lumbar disc herniation or bulge
  • Lumbar facet syndrome
  • Sacroiliac (SI) joint dysfunction
  • Piriformis syndrome
  • Spinal stenosis
  • Postural imbalance and muscle guarding
  • Degenerative disc disease

Sciatica

When the pain shoots down your leg

Sciatica isn't a diagnosis — it's a symptom. That sharp, burning, or electric pain that runs from your low back through the glute and down the leg follows the sciatic nerve, and it's telling you something is compressing or irritating it.

The two most common culprits are a herniated lumbar disc and piriformis syndrome. They require different treatment approaches, which is why a proper evaluation matters before anyone touches your spine.

Chiropractic care addresses sciatic pain by reducing nerve compression at the source — not masking it with medication or waiting it out. For disc-related sciatica, spinal decompression therapy can be particularly effective, creating negative intradiscal pressure that allows the disc to retract and the nerve to breathe.

You might have sciatica if you experience:

  • Sharp or burning pain from the low back into the glute or leg
  • Numbness, tingling, or weakness in the leg or foot
  • Pain that worsens with sitting, bending, or lifting
  • Pain that improves when walking or lying down
  • One-sided symptoms (usually affects one leg)

Treatment

What your care plan looks like

Every plan is built around your specific findings — not a template. That said, most low back and sciatica cases at Pinnacle involve some combination of the following:

01

Thorough evaluation

Orthopedic and neurological testing, postural assessment, and X-rays when clinically indicated. We don't skip this — it's what makes everything else work.

02

Chiropractic adjustment

Targeted spinal manipulation to restore joint mobility, reduce nerve irritation, and break the pain-spasm cycle. Specific to your findings, not a one-size-fits-all protocol.

03

Spinal decompression (when indicated)

Non-surgical mechanical traction for disc-related cases. Creates negative intradiscal pressure to allow retraction of herniated or bulging disc material and reduce nerve compression.

04

Soft tissue therapy

Targeted work on the muscles, fascia, and supporting structures that contribute to pain and movement restriction — piriformis, QL, hip flexors, and more.

05

Corrective exercise

A home exercise program built around your specific dysfunction — not generic core work. Designed to reinforce the structural corrections made in-office so results last.
