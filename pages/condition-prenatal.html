Prenatal Care

What chiropractic does during pregnancy

Pregnancy creates significant and rapid changes to the musculoskeletal system. The center of gravity shifts forward, the lumbar curve increases, the pelvis widens, and the ligaments that stabilize the joints — particularly the sacroiliac joints and pubic symphysis — become more lax under the influence of relaxin. These changes are normal, but they place the spine and pelvis under mechanical stress that can cause significant discomfort.

Chiropractic care during pregnancy addresses these structural changes directly. It isn't about treating the pregnancy — it's about maintaining proper spinal and pelvic alignment so the body can adapt to the changes of pregnancy with less pain, better mobility, and less nerve interference. Most pregnant patients are surprised by how much better they feel after even a few visits.

Care is safe throughout all trimesters. Our treatment table has adjustable sections to accommodate a growing belly, and all techniques are modified for pregnancy. We work collaboratively with your OB or midwife and can communicate with your provider as needed.

Webster Technique

What it is and how it works

The Webster Technique is a specific chiropractic analysis and adjustment protocol developed by Dr. Larry Webster and certified through the International Chiropractic Pediatric Association (ICPA). It focuses on the sacrum and surrounding structures — analyzing sacral alignment, identifying areas of joint restriction, and addressing related soft tissue tension in the round ligaments and hip flexors.

The goal of the Webster Technique is to reduce sacral subluxation and improve pelvic function — creating what the ICPA describes as a state of "neuro-biomechanical function in the pelvis." When the pelvis is balanced and the soft tissues are free of tension, the uterus has the space it needs to expand symmetrically, and the baby has the room and the environment to move into an optimal position for birth.

Webster Technique is not an external version — it does not manually move the baby. It creates the physiological conditions that support optimal fetal positioning. Research published in the Journal of Manipulative and Physiological Therapeutics reported an 82% success rate in resolving breech presentation when Webster Technique was initiated before 36 weeks.

Reasons patients seek Webster Technique:

  • Baby in breech, transverse, or oblique position
  • Pelvic pain or asymmetry during pregnancy
  • Wanting to optimize conditions for vaginal birth
  • History of difficult labor or posterior presentation in prior pregnancies
  • Midwife or OB recommendation
  • General pregnancy wellness and discomfort reduction

Treatment

What prenatal care at Pinnacle looks like

Prenatal chiropractic visits are gentle, specific, and adapted to each stage of pregnancy. Most visits are 30–45 minutes, and the treatment table is equipped with a drop-away section so you can lie comfortably face-down even in the third trimester.

01

Prenatal evaluation

A thorough history including obstetric history, current symptoms, and any provider notes. Postural assessment, pelvic alignment analysis, sacral and lumbar evaluation, and soft tissue assessment. We communicate with your OB or midwife as needed and coordinate care appropriately.

02

Webster-specific sacral analysis

Assessment of sacral alignment, leg length, and iliac crest symmetry. Identification of the primary sacral subluxation and associated soft tissue restrictions — the two components the Webster adjustment addresses.

03

Sacral adjustment

Gentle, specific adjustment to the sacrum using light pressure and a drop-table technique. Safe at all stages of pregnancy. The adjustment addresses joint restriction and restores normal sacropelvic mechanics — not a forceful manipulation.

04

Round ligament and soft tissue release

Gentle myofascial work on the round ligaments and related hip and pelvic soft tissues. This is the second component of the Webster protocol — releasing the soft tissue tension that can restrict uterine space and contribute to pelvic asymmetry.

05

Pregnancy-safe exercise & guidance

Targeted home recommendations for pelvic stability, daily positioning, and movement habits that support your care between visits. Coordinated with your current stage of pregnancy and activity level.

When to Start

What trimester should I begin?

The short answer: whenever you're pregnant and want to feel better. There's no wrong trimester to start. Each stage of pregnancy brings different demands on the body, and chiropractic care is beneficial throughout.

For patients specifically seeking Webster Technique for fetal positioning, starting by the beginning of the third trimester (around 28–32 weeks) gives the most time to work before position becomes critical. For patients with breech presentation at 32–35 weeks, we prioritize care and increase visit frequency to maximize the window before delivery.

Postpartum care is also available and often underutilized. The delivery process — especially long labors, vacuum or forceps deliveries, or cesarean sections — creates significant stress on the pelvic floor, sacrum, and lumbar spine. A postpartum evaluation within the first few weeks after delivery can address these issues before they become chronic problems.
